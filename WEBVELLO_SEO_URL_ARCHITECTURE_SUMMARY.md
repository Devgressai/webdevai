## Indexing Strategy Summary

### Tier 1: Always Index (~2,000-3,000 pages)
- Core pages
- Service pages
- City hub pages
- City × Tier 1 Service pages (872 pages)
- Blog posts
- Case studies
- Tools
- Resources
- Glossary entries
- Entity pages

### Tier 2: Conditional Index (Variable)
- City × Industry × Service pages (if has CMS content OR unique 2000+ word content)
- Research studies (if substantial content)
- Extended comparison pages (if substantial content)

### Tier 3: Noindex (~1,744+ pages)
- City × Industry hub pages (navigation/bridge pages)
- City × Industry × Service pages (if no unique content)
- Thin comparison pages
- Navigation-only pages

## Internal Linking Rules

### Hub Pages Link To:
- Relevant cluster pages
- Relevant service pages
- Top city pages (10-20)
- Top industry pages
- Related case studies
- Related tools
- Related blog posts (5-10)

### Service Pages Link To:
- Parent service hub
- Related service pages (3-5)
- Top city pages for that service (5-10)
- Top industry pages for that service (3-5)
- Relevant case studies (2-3)
- Relevant tools (1-2)
- Relevant blog posts (3-5)

### City Pages Link To:
- Top services for that city (8 Tier 1 services)
- Top industries for that city (3-5)
- State page
- Related city pages (3-5)
- Relevant case studies (1-2)
- Relevant blog posts (2-3)

### Programmatic Pages Link To:
- Parent city page
- Parent service page
- Parent industry page (if applicable)
- Related programmatic pages (2-3)
- Relevant case studies (1-2)
